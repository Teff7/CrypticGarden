How cryptic crosswords work (and how this game teaches them)

Each clue has two halves:

  • Definition — plain meaning of the answer.
  • Wordplay — instructions to build the answer (e.g., anagram, container, hidden).

Use the Hints menu if you’re learning:

  • Definition highlights the definition part.
  • Reveal a letter fills a random empty cell in the entry.
  • Reveal the trick colours and tool-tips the indicator and fodder.

Click a numbered square to switch between Across and Down on that start square. Type moves the cursor; Backspace moves back. When an entry is correct, it locks in. “Reveal answer” will fill it.

Ready to try building clues yourself? Keep these common devices and sample clues in mind while you solve:

Anagram
Letters are mixed to form the answer. Look for signals like broken, drunk, mixed, wild. Example: Inebriated pirates travel about (7) → TRAIPSE (anagram of PIRATES).
Reversal
Read letters backwards. Common signals: returned, rising, back, up. Example: Spies bring silverware back (6) → SNOOPS (SPOONS reversed).
Container (Insertion)
One word goes inside another. Indicators include in, around, holding. Sometimes the clue also tells you to reverse the container or contents first. Example: Uncovered calf in grass (8) → REVEALED (VEAL inside REED).
Hidden Word
Answer is hidden inside the clue. Indicators: in part, within, concealed. Sometimes paired with a reversal signal. Example: A long time in a dungeon (3) → EON (hidden in “dungEON”).
Charade
Parts are placed side by side to make the answer. Example: Outlaw leader managing money (7) → BANKING (BAN + KING).
Acrostic (Initials/Finals)
Take first or last letters. Indicators: initially, at first, finally. Example: At first, Actor Needing New Identity Emulates Orphan (5) → ANNIE (initial letters).
Double Definition
Two distinct meanings point to one word. Example: Cover for the head / gangster (4) → HOOD.
Homophone
Sounds like another word. Indicators: heard, we’re told, reportedly. Example: The king has no successor, we hear (4) → BALD (sounds like “no heir”).
Deletion
Letters are removed. Indicators: endless, heartless, beheaded, topless. Example: Bird is cowardly, about to fly away (5) → RAVEN (CRAVEN minus C).
&lit (All-in-One / Literally)
Whole clue serves as both definition and wordplay. Example: Leaders of Iranian mosques and Muslim sects! (5) → IMAMS (first letters; the full clue defines it too).
